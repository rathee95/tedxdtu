Take a T.R.I.P

Presenting the story of Indranil Sarkar for the "Xpress Your Story" campaign.

February 27, 2016 TEDxDTU

Presenting to you the third story of the "Xpress Your Story" campaign, Indranil Sarkar from the Manipal School of Communication talks about how journalism induced motivation in him to make a change for the rural population in India. Title: Take a T.R.I.P "In December 2014, I was doing an internship with Times of India, Nagpur. During that time I got the opportunity to visit a remote village in Vidarbh region of Maharashtra, called Yavatmal. Unfortunately, that village is also one of the highest farmer suicide prone areas in the country. I got to know a lot of stories during my stay there. But one story that changed everything was when I came to know most of all the farmers in that region did not commit suicide on their own, they were encouraged by their own family members to do so, as the Government fails to pay a compensation when there is a drought, but the same government promptly pays a lump sum hen there is a death. After listening to this story, I was perturbed by the situation. At the same time I was extremely ashamed that in spite of being a journalism student and in spite of being exposed to so many media everyday I never knew about such stories. That very moment I decided something needs to be done. Thus, The Rural India Project(TRIP) was born with the whole and sole intention to tell the world about the real and unheard stories of the rural India. 70% of the Indian population still resides in the villages but how many stories of these people reaches the media? These are the people who are working day in and day out to put the meals on our table. But, unfortunately we, who reside in the urban part of the country, have almost forgotten this very fact. Through this project we want remind the nation we are nothing without them, they are the backbone of the country and they deserve much better than what they are getting. We at TRIP want to be the voices of these people. Less than a year since the initiative, we have brought forward stories from various parts of India including Jharia, Mcleodganj, parts of North East and few other places. We also hope by telling these stories, one day we will be able to erase the boundary between the rich and the poor and make way for a more unified and better India."

About TEDx, x = independently organized event
In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. At a TEDx event, TEDTalks video and live speakers combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. These local, self-organized events are branded TEDx, where x = independently organized TED event. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events are self-organized(subject to certain rules and regulations).
